Animals in the jungle

Get into the world of Animals

  2. Aquatic

Lion

Lions are large carnivorous mammals. They are part of the Felidae family, commonly known as cats. Lions are known for their majestic appearance and distinctive manes.

Tiger

Tigers are the largest species of the cat family. They are known for their distinctive orange coat with black stripes. Tigers are solitary animals and are highly territorial.

Bear

Bears are large mammals belonging to the family Ursidae. They are typically characterized by a large body, stocky build, and shaggy hair. Bears are omnivores, feeding on both plants and animals.

Terrestrial Animals

Terrestrial animals are animals that live predominantly or entirely on land (e.g. cats, chickens, ants, spiders), as compared with aquatic animals, which live predominantly or entirely in the water (e.g. fish, lobsters, octopuses), and amphibians, which rely on aquatic and terrestrial habitats (e.g. frogs and newts).

Aquatic Animals

An aquatic animal is any animal, whether vertebrate or invertebrate, that lives in water for all or most of its lifetime. Many insects such as mosquitoes, mayflies, dragonflies and caddisflies have aquatic larvae, with winged adults.
